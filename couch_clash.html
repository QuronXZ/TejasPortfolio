Overview

Title: Couch Clash

Engine: Unity

Time to design and develop: 2 weeks

Team size: 4

Genre: Local 1v1 Fighting / Party Game

Description: Couch Clash is a fast, goofy, and nostalgic local multiplayer fighting game that merges two worlds: 2D combat inspired by retro arcade brawlers and 3D couch chaos representing real-life player mischief.

Core Gameplay

Core Mechanic: It‚Äôs a split-reality fighting game where two players battle inside a 2D pixel arena; just like playing another Mortal Kombat or Tekken; while their real-world avatars sitting on a couch can physically interfere through 3D interactions.

ATTACK

Attack in the 2D world just like you would in Mortal Kombat. Spam punches, land perfect kicks, and feel that classic arcade style rush.

DISTRACT

Use sneaky 3D combos to distract your opponent in the real world. Push, fart, and pull off goofy pranks to stun them in 2D.

OVERPOWER

When your opponent‚Äôs stunned, it‚Äôs your moment to strike back and dominate! Turn playful chaos into a knockout victory.

Game Design Process

Our main challenge was that Couch Clash isn‚Äôt just one game; it‚Äôs two things happening at once: a 2D PvP fight and a 3D distraction game, played by two players simultaneously. Balancing attention and enjoyment between these layers was the toughest part.

Cognitive Load Study

The challenge:

  • Both worlds are equally fun, so players care to engage in each.
  • It doesn‚Äôt turn into parallel play; players shouldn‚Äôt focus on only 2D or only 3D.
  • It feels fair, while still delivering the thrill of unfair play that defines our design goal.

Visual Representation

We designed the visual focus intentionally. About 70% attention on the 3D scene (the players and their actions) and 30% on the 2D fight, since players would naturally fixate on the in-game battle.

  • Instant clarity: One frame communicates both worlds at once.
  • Shared focus: Players stay aware of both 2D and 3D actions.
  • Natural emotion: The scene itself tells the story; no UI needed.

Playtesting iterations

  • Improved UI for better clarity and feedback.

Introduced icons alongside text to make the interface more readable and intuitive. The attack cooldown was redesigned for clearer visibility and faster player recognition.

  • Upgraded 3D Character Models

The old skinny avatars were replaced with fully realized 3D characters, each designed with distinct personalities and animations that reflect their attack styles.

  • Key Learnings

1. Balancing the Game:
Creating both a 2D brawler and a 3D distraction system was tough. Balancing them so both felt equally fun and fair was a major learning.

2. Seamless Integration:
I learned how to make two different worlds feel like one continuous experience, where 2D and 3D interactions naturally flow together.

3. Rendering 2D into 3D:
Figuring out how to display the 2D game within the 3D world taught me how design and tech can merge to create a cohesive space.
