Overview

Title: Lab Genesis

Type: Board Game

Time to design and develop: 2 weeks

Team size: 3

Genre: Science, Genetics, Strategy

Description: Visionary geneticists compete for ultimate glory in this strategic board game. Splice DNA from extraordinary creatures to build your lab, outmaneuver rival scientists to secure vital government funding, and become the first to claim the Nobel Prize in genetic science.

Core Gameplay

Components:

A collection of hexagonal habitat tiles, 3 unique player mats, and a pool of colorful gene strands.

Setup:

Place the hex tiles randomly in a clustered hex shape with the Government tile in the center. Place two of the same gene strands randomly on each habitat tile. Players start by placing their chosen animal on any tile along the board's edge.

Move patterns:

Each animal has a unique pattern. The Elephant marches in a straight line, the Kangaroo bounds in a zig-zag, and the Horse moves in a classic L-shape.

Playtesting Iterations

Beta Version

The original game challenged players to build a research lab and secure three government fundings to win the Nobel Prize. Gameplay involved resource gathering and strategic encounters, but proved overly complex and lengthy during testing.

Key Feedback

Playtesters reported the rules were hard to learn, gameplay was too long, and some animal abilities felt unbalanced. The player mats also needed better layout for usability.

We did total nine iterations, experimenting and speculating with different mechanics and play cases.

Final Design

The refined version simplifies victory: first to earn 5 Victory Points by completing mutation missions wins. The clearer focus on using animal abilities to collect genes made the game faster and more engaging. Mission cards and player mats were redesigned for clarity, and all animal powers were rebalanced for fairer competition.

  • The finalized instruction manual, as shipped with the game.

  • Key Learnings

1. Value of Player Feedback:
The most significant reward was positive player reception, with many expressing a desire to replay the game. This confirmed that a fun, engaging core loop is the true foundation of a successful design.

2. Strength of Iterative Design:
The process demonstrated how a single, well-crafted mechanic can be refined to create deep and varied gameplay, proving that strategic depth comes from thoughtful iteration, not unnecessary complexity.

3. Critical Role of Playtesting:
External playtesting proved essential, as it consistently revealed unforeseen flaws and balance issues. This underscored that a game must be shaped by its players to achieve a polished and balanced final state.
